01 — The Academy

The fall off-season is a mess.
We fixed it.

The fall “off” season leading into the January–July AAU season has become chaos — crazy travel costs, high stress "circuits" or uninspiring rec leagues, and individual training that won’t actually prepare your child for the team season.

The Takeover Basketball Academy turns the fall into an ideal preseason — one organized, high-level experience that builds the foundation for a successful year inside the TTO Grassroots family.

It’s built on five pillars:

01
Strength & Conditioning
Build the engine — athleticism, durability, and work capacity.
02
Skill Development
Proper technique taught so players can train effectively on their own.
03
Physical & Mental Toughness
Compete hard, respond to adversity, and grow a winner’s mindset.
04
Competition
30 focused players per grade create a high-level, intense atmosphere.
05
Basketball IQ
Film study, situational learning, and reads that translate to real games.
02 — How it works

Three ways we develop. One capstone tryout.

~30 hours of programming across eight weeks — on the court, on Zoom, and in real competition.

Sundays · 16 hrs
Academy Skills Sessions

Eight 2-hour sessions led by TTO coaches at rotating DMV venues. High-intensity skill work and conditioning, split by grade (9–11am & 11am–1pm).

Wednesdays · 8 hrs
Wisdom Wednesdays

Weekly 1-hour Zoom sessions on nutrition, stretching & recovery, and film study — plus parent education with HS coaches, admissions staff, and TTO executives.

Saturdays · 6 hrs
October Game Days

Two Saturdays of competitive, refereed game play to apply the work — real reps against real competition.

The Capstone
November Tryouts — your inside track

The Academy is an extended evaluation. Players are coached by the same staff making roster decisions — the best on-ramp to a 2026–27 TTO Grassroots team.

06 — Questions

Frequently asked

Where and when will sessions take place?+

Sundays from Sep 13 to Nov 1, at rotating DMV venues — we partner with local universities (Georgetown, GWU, Howard, George Mason, American) and top prep schools. Venues are confirmed and released ahead of each block. AM/PM blocks alternate by grade each week.

Is there a discount for returning players?+

Yes — returning Academy families save $50 ($325 instead of $375) when they register by August 28, 2026. After that date, the full $375 rate applies.

If my child can’t make every session, is there a discount?+

Because you’re reserving a seat in the class, no. We encourage full attendance, but even at half the sessions the per-hour value (~$10–12/hr for high-level training) is exceptional.

Does the Academy guarantee a spot on a Takeover team?+

No — but it’s the strongest inside track. Our coaches run the Academy, so your child gets an “extended tryout” to impress them. Historically there are about two teams (22 spots) per grade. If a player isn’t selected, we work with partner organizations to find the right fit.

How do I register and pay?+

Complete the registration form below — payment is collected directly in the TeamSnap form, no separate link is sent.

Is registration first-come, first-served?+

Yes — 30 seats per grade. If a class is oversubscribed, an evaluation at the first session determines who advances; any player who doesn’t advance is refunded their $200 deposit in full.

What grades is the Academy for?+

Players in 3rd through 6th grade. Sessions are grouped 3rd/4th and 5th/6th.
